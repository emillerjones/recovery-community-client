I think we should make this a real artifact for the project. Not "notes," but the document that defines the brand.

I'd structure it something like this:

Recovery Community Platform
Brand Vision & Creative Direction
Working Draft v1
1. Executive Summary
What we're building.
Why it exists.
The emotional goal.
2. The Problem

Most recovery organizations focus on quitting.

This platform focuses on maintaining recovery.

The product isn't cannabis.

The product isn't treatment.

The product is community.

3. Core Philosophy

Recovery isn't a finish line.

It's something you tend every day.

Just like a fire.

4. The Fire Metaphor

This was probably the biggest breakthrough.

Not:

cannabis leaf
tree
forest

Instead...

A fire.

People gather around it.

It gives warmth.

It gives hope.

It gives light.

It has to be tended.

If everyone contributes...

The fire grows.

5. Emotional Journey

When someone first arrives...

They're alone.

Walking through darkness.

Not scary darkness.

Quiet darkness.

Eventually...

They see light through the trees.

People talking.

People laughing.

A fire.

That's the community.

6. Logged-in Experience

Everything reinforces the metaphor.

Unread messages

→ sparks

More members online

→ brighter fire

Helping someone

→ another log

Community milestones

→ larger fire

Recovery streak

→ your ember grows

The entire product becomes

Keeping the fire alive.

7. Mobile Experience

One of my favorite ideas.

Black screen.

Very subtle ember.

Smoke drifting upward.

Tiny glowing coals.

Swipe.

The fire ignites.

The site comes alive.

Not flashy.

Ceremonial.

Almost meditative.

8. Brand Personality

Warm.

Hopeful.

Quiet.

Comforting.

Timeless.

Natural.

Human.

Never corporate.

Never "startup."

Never clinical.

10. Inspiration

Not websites.

Experiences.

Walking through a forest.

Finding a camp.

A lighthouse.

A lantern.

A cabin.

Friends around a bonfire.

Keeping watch through the night.

Why Watchfire stood out

Historically...

A watchfire stayed lit through the night.

It meant:

People are here.

You're safe.

Come join us.

That symbolism fits the community remarkably well.

12. Slogan Exploration

Community for Maintaining Recovery

Recovery Happens Together

Keep the Fire Alive

You Don't Have to Walk Alone.

Find Your People.

Keep Hope Burning.

Recovery is a Journey.

Keep Growing.

13. UX Principles

The website shouldn't feel like software.

It should feel like arriving somewhere.

No hard edges.

No loud gradients.

No neon.

No tech aesthetic.

Instead...

Warmth.

Texture.

Atmosphere.

14. Things We Rejected

Recovery With The Exit Drug as the brand name.

Too long.

Too difficult to say.

Cannabis-first branding.

Too narrow.

Corporate wellness.

Too generic.

Forest for the sake of forest.

Nature should support the story.

Not become the story.

15. North Star

This is the line I think should end the document.

The fire isn't the community.

The people are.

The fire is simply the light they keep alive together.

I actually think this is the first document I'd put in the root of the project, alongside the README. It's no longer just brainstorming—it captures the philosophy behind the platform. Whether you ask Claude, hire a designer, or come back to this in two years, everyone starts from the same vision instead of reinventing it.
